← Corey Scher

I develop scientific methods and datasets to help better understand, respond to, and recover from the physical impacts of armed conflict and disaster. I work to support a broad range of downstream applications including humanitarian response, data journalism, international law, and academic research. With Jamon Van Den Hoek and Conflict Ecology at Oregon State, I run an open-source building damage assessment program. Our deliverables across multiple conflict and disaster settings have been featured in hundreds of news articles, referenced in reports from humanitarian organizations, and cited in international legal proceedings.

I hold a PhD in Earth and Environmental Sciences from the CUNY Graduate Center, where my dissertation examined the physical and environmental impacts of armed conflict using synthetic aperture radar interferometry.
