I develop scientific methods and datasets to help better understand, respond to, and recover from the physical impacts of armed conflict and disaster. I work to support downstream applications including humanitarian response, data journalism, international law, and academic research.

With Jamon Van Den Hoek and Conflict Ecology at Oregon State, I run an open-source building damage assessment program. Our deliverables across multiple conflict and disaster settings have informed hundreds of news articles, been referenced in reports from humanitarian organizations, and cited in international legal proceedings.
